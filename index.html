History of Editing

Early Film Editing: In the 1900s, editors used scissors to physically cut and splice film strips, taping pieces together by hand. Moviola machines allowed frame-by-frame editing.

Digital Editing: In the 1990s, non-linear editing systems replaced physical cutting. Editors now use software like Adobe Premiere to arrange scenes digitally.

How It Works

Film Editing: Rolls of film were threaded through machines like the Moviola to manually locate and cut scenes.

Digital Editing: Footage is imported to software, where editors arrange, cut, and enhance clips digitally.
